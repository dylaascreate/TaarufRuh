Here is a concise, structured breakdown of TaarufRuh to serve as your project’s core documentation:

1. The Desired Problem to Solve
During Ramadan, many Muslims experience a high surge of motivation that slowly fades, leading to the "mid-Ramadan slump." People struggle to maintain istiqamah (consistency) across all aspects of their faith. Furthermore, while it's easy to track tangible actions like solat (prayer), Muslims often lack a structured way to measure and encourage proactive social duties (Habluminannas), such as infaq (charity), dakwah (preaching), or mencegah kemungkaran (preventing bad deeds).

2. The Solution
TaarufRuh is a React Native mobile application that digitizes and gamifies muhasabah (self-reflection). By allowing users to select a target "spiritual tier," the app generates a holistic daily checklist of both personal worship and community actions. It transforms the abstract concept of "nourishing the soul" into measurable, visually rewarding daily habits, helping users stay accountable without feeling overwhelmed.

3. The Goals
Encourage Consistency: Help users build sustainable, long-term Islamic habits that last beyond Ramadan.

Promote Holistic Worship: Ensure users balance their relationship with God (prayer, Quran) with their relationship with the community (charity, advising others).

Positive Reinforcement: Use gamification (leveling up) to motivate users through progress rather than guilt over missed obligations.

4. Theme and Colors
The UI should feel serene, pure, and motivating, avoiding visual clutter that might cause stress.

Theme: Minimalist, modern Islamic, and tranquil.

Primary Color: Deep Emerald Green (representing growth, life, and Islamic tradition) or Midnight Navy Blue (evoking the peace of the night sky and Tahajjud prayers).

Accent Color: Warm Gold or Brass (used sparingly for progress bars, level-up badges, and highlights to signify value and reward).

Background: Soft Off-White or Light Sand (for readability and a clean, pure aesthetic).

5. Core Pages (Screens)
Onboarding & Niat (Intention) Setup: Where the user selects their starting spiritual tier (e.g., Beginner, Intermediate, Advanced) to tailor their daily tasks.

Daily Dashboard (Home): The main hub showing today's date, prayer times, a quick-view progress ring, and immediate tasks to tick off.

Muhasabah Log (The Tracker): A detailed, categorized checklist to input specific numbers (e.g., pages of Quran read, amount of infaq given, number of Islamic lectures attended).

Soul Analytics (Progress Page): A visual representation of the user's journey. Displays their current "Level," historical streaks, and charts showing which areas (e.g., dakwah vs. solat sunat) need more attention.

Inspiration Hub: A small feed of daily Quranic verses, Hadith, or practical ideas on how to fulfill the mencegah kemungkaran or dakwah tasks for the day.

6. Key Functions
Dynamic Task Generation: Adjusts the difficulty and quantity of daily goals based on the user's chosen level.

XP & Leveling System: Grants experience points for logging completed deeds, triggering a visual "level up" to reward consistency.

Custom Counters: Allows users to input exact metrics (e.g., logging exactly 5 pages of the Quran instead of just a simple checkbox).

Daily Reset & Streaks: Automatically refreshes the checklist each day at Maghrib or Fajr, while keeping a tally of consecutive active days.
